Excel as a Leader with Self-Reflection

I try to conduct reviews with my team members as often as I can. And when I do, it is not only about their performance, but also mine.


It is important to get feedback or evaluated by your team members because that is the best way to know if you are performing up to your team members expectations.


Why is it important to reflect on yourself as a leader? Because, how do you expect your team members to be at their best if you are not being the best for them?


Because at the end of the day, you are not only responsible of delivering results, but also to support your team members to be the best version of themselves.


More often than not, a team's performance is highly dependent on the leader of the team.

This is what I usually do:

  1. Conduct the evaluation during their review because they are very unlikely to walk up to you and tell you what you did wrongly or what you can do better.

  2. I ask questions that makes it easier for them to open up. For example, "How do you think I can improve to help you and the team work effectively?"

  3. After receiving feedback, I will update them how are we going change things and eventually help the team work better together - and of course I would have to walk the talk.

In summary, if your team is not performing, you should reflect on things that you could have done better.

Hence, I think that managers should also be evaluated by their subordinates.

After all, relationship works both ways right?
